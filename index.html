The provided texts are metadata records from the Smithsonian's Freer Gallery of Art and Arthur M. Sackler Gallery, describing three distinct but related blue-and-white porcelain pieces from a five-piece garniture. Each record details the object's physical characteristics, provenance, and history of ownership, tracing their journey from the Duveen Brothers to the Freer Gallery. The objects, created in Jingdezhen, China during the Qing dynasty's Kangxi reign, are described as including vases and beakers, with intricate cobalt blue underglaze decorations of court scenes and floral motifs. The records also note the digital 3D models available of the objects and specify usage rights.

Collection 1 - Beaker shaped vase

Introduce

This is a beaker-shaped vase, one of two in a five-piece garniture. It is made of white porcelain with a clear, feldspathic glaze. The vase is decorated with underglaze cobalt blue painting depicting a court scene.It was created in Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, China, during the Kangxi reign (1662-1722) of the Qing dynasty.

A garniture is a set of decorative vases or other objects, typically made of porcelain or ceramic, intended to be displayed together. This vase was part of a five-piece garniture, meaning it was originally displayed with four other matching pieces. The vase is decorated in underglaze cobalt blue with a court scene similar to that found on the baluster vases in the same garniture. It also depicts court ladies in a garden setting. The different scenes are divided by herringbone borders.

Collection 2 - Covered baluster vase

Introduce

This object is a covered baluster vase, one of three in a five-piece garniture. It is made of white porcelain with a clear, feldspathic glaze. The vase is decorated with underglaze cobalt blue painting. The main scene depicts a court lady being entertained by a dancer and musicians. The neck of the vase is decorated with floral sprays, and the domed cover features ladies, flowers, rocks, and a bud-shaped finial knob. This vase dates back to the Kangxi reign (1662-1722) of the Qing dynasty in China. It is a prime example of Jingdezhen ware, known for its high quality and beautiful blue-and-white decoration. The vase's provenance includes prominent collectors such as Duveen Brothers, Mrs. B. F. Jones, and Myron S. Falk, Sr.

This vase was made in Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, China, a renowned center for porcelain production since the Yuan dynasty.

The assembled vase, including the cover, has a height of 45.5 cm (17 15/16 in) and a diameter of 17.1 cm (6 3/4 in).A garniture is a set of decorative objects, typically vases, designed to be displayed together. This vase was part of a five-piece garniture, which likely included two other covered baluster vases and two beaker-shaped vases.

Collection 3 - Blue and white vases

Introduce

The vase is made of porcelain with a cobalt blue pigment under a clear glaze.

This vase was made in Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, China, a renowned center for porcelain production since the Yuan dynasty.The vase is 44.5 cm (17 1/2 in) high and has a diameter of 21.4 cm (8 7/16 in).

The vase is crafted in the Jingdezhen ware style, a type of porcelain known for its high quality and intricate designs.

The vase is decorated with a large, loose lotus scroll on each of its three sections. A band filled with a diaper pattern encircles the central lobed section. The design was inspired by ancient bronze Ku vessels.

The vase was made in Jingdezhen, Jiangxi province, China, during the Kangxi period of the Qing dynasty. This dates the vase to the mid-17th to early 18th century.
